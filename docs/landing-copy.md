## SECTION 2 — The Problem (fear framing)

### The call every business owner dreads

It usually starts with a customer: *"Hey, I tried to visit your website and it's showing some weird error."*

By then, it's already been down for hours — maybe days. Every person who searched for you in that time found a broken page and moved on to a competitor.

Here's what most business owners don't know until it's too late:

- **Websites don't maintain themselves.** The software behind your site releases security patches constantly. Skip them, and you're leaving the door unlocked.
- **Hackers don't target big companies — they target easy ones.** Automated bots scan thousands of small business sites a day looking for outdated software. It's nothing personal. It's just easy.
- **This isn't scare talk — it's measured.** Security researchers at Patchstack logged roughly 6,700 new WordPress vulnerabilities in just six months, and found that about 4 in 10 were exploitable in real-world attacks. And when sites do get hacked, the pattern is overwhelming: industry analyses have found that **61% of compromised WordPress sites were running outdated software** at the time of the attack. Staying updated isn't housekeeping. It's the whole game.
- **An emergency cleanup is expensive.** Agencies typically charge $500–$1,500 to recover a hacked site — if your data can be recovered at all. Without a recent backup, years of work can vanish permanently.
- **When it breaks, who do you even call?** If the answer is "the person who built it years ago" or "my nephew," you don't have a plan. You have a hope.

**For less than the cost of one emergency, your site can be monitored, backed up, and protected every single day.**

## SECTION 3 — How It Works (simplicity + relief)

### You run your business. We'll run your website.

**1. We connect your site (Day 1).** You give us access, we take a full backup immediately, and hook your site into our monitoring systems. From that moment, it's checked every 5 minutes, around the clock.

**2. We protect it quietly, every week.** Software updates, security scans, fresh backups, performance checks. Updates are tested on a private copy of your site *before* they touch the live one — because an untested update is the #1 way "maintenance" takes a site down. And your backups are stored off your server, in separate secure storage. (A backup that lives on a hacked server is no backup at all.) You won't notice a thing — that's the point.

**3. You get proof, every month.** A plain-English report showing exactly what we updated, blocked, backed up, and fixed. You'll always know what you're paying for.
